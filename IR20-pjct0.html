My Process

Research

The 2020 Impact Report for the University of Colorado is a multifaceted project aimed to communicate the breadth and depth of the impact of giving to the University. This is a collection of facts, stats and stories of people doing great things thanks to philanthropy at the University. THis project was a collaboration between the Marketing and Communication and the Digital Engagement team. My role as lead designer consisted of creating the visual strategy, UI/UX, graphics and print material production.

Ideation

Working in the context of COVID, I strategized with our team’s story producer to create a content strategy that allowed for a variety of media sources. Due to social distancing laws we couldn’t rely on professional photography and had to create a visual framework that would connect all the content why accommodating for quality discrepancy. I used moodboards and visual strategy pitch decks to communicate my ideas about the direction to the team.

Wireframes

I worked closely with the content strategist and the developers to ensure that the structure of our site would allow for content variation for segmented audiences, testing and meeting accessibility standards. Creating many versions of wireframes to discuss the pros and cons of different variations and bring the team to consensus. Finally using the wireframes to work with our contract developers to ensure the project remained in budget.

Design

The site design was based on the need for modularity in content from story to story, a variation in range for the numbers of stories and the ability to vary the layout based on audience segmentation. The visual design was intended to work with a variety of media and communicate an uplifting narrative.

Prototypes

Working from concept to final production, I used sketch and invision to create visual mockups of desktop and mobile renditions of the sites pages and send the design components to the developer.

Conclusion

This project consisted primarily of the site but it was created in conjunction with social distribution kits, email campaigns and print collateral.
